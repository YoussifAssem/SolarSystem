Sun:
It is a yellow dwarf star, a hot ball of glowing
gases at the heart of our solar system. It is a
nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, heated to
incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core,
radiating the energy mainly as visible light and infrared
radiation. It is by far the most important source of energy
for life on Earth. Its gravity holds the solar system together
keeping everything in its orbit.
